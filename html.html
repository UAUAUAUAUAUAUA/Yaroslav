Emerging and Regional Economies Drive Highest Percentage Growth, Outpacing Established Global Leaders from 2020 to 2025.

A comparative analysis of projected GDP growth across three distinct segments—Top 20 Global Economies, Eastern European nations, and the Baltic States—highlights a fundamental disparity between absolute size and percentage growth. While large economies like the US drive the greatest nominal dollar increase, the highest percentage expansion comes from high-potential markets. Specifically, the smaller, agile Baltic states and select Eastern European countries are projected to achieve rapid convergence, demonstrating significantly higher growth rates than many established world economic powers, making them key areas for future investment focus.
